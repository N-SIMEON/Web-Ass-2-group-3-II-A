# Problem Statement — AgriStack Irish Potato Marketplace

## Project Overview
**AgriStack** is a cooperative listing and bulk order management system for Irish potato farmers in Musanze District, Northern Province, Rwanda.

---

## The Street Problem

> *"We grow the food that feeds Kigali, but middlemen take most of the profit. By the time our potatoes reach the market, we have already sold them at a fraction of the real price."*
> — Jean Uwimana, potato farmer, Kinigi Sector, Musanze (interviewed March 2024)

### Core Problem
Farmers and cooperatives in Musanze District lose significant income because:

1. **Price Manipulation** — Local middlemen (negociants) buy in bulk at artificially low prices (150–200 RWF/kg) and resell in Kigali markets at 300–400 RWF/kg, capturing most of the value chain margin.

2. **Information Asymmetry** — Farmers have no visibility into real market prices. They accept whatever price middlemen offer because they lack direct contact with bulk buyers like supermarkets, hotels, schools, and wholesalers.

3. **No Direct Channel** — There is no digital platform enabling cooperatives to list their harvests with transparent pricing and connect directly with aggregators and bulk buyers.

4. **Lost Harvest Value** — Without timely buyers, potatoes rot (shelf life ~2 weeks post-harvest). Farmers are forced to sell immediately at low prices rather than wait for better deals.

5. **Opacity in Transactions** — There is no audit trail of who bought what, at what price, and from which farm. This makes fraud easy and dispute resolution impossible.

---

## Context

- **Location:** Musanze District, Northern Province, Rwanda
- **Crop:** Irish Potatoes (Solanum tuberosum) — Musanze produces ~40% of Rwanda's total potato output
- **Key Sectors:** Kinigi, Cyuve, Shingiro, Gataraga, Nyange, Remera, Muhoza
- **Key Actors:** Smallholder farmers, cooperatives (SACCOs), aggregators, urban wholesalers, supermarkets

---

## Impact of Problem
- Average farmer income loss: estimated 30–50% of potential value per harvest season
- Cooperative members often earn less than cost of production in off-peak seasons
- Youth are leaving farming due to poor profitability
- Food waste due to unsold/undervalued harvests

---

## Proposed Solution
A web-based marketplace (AgriStack) with:
- Role-based access for Farmers, Buyers, and Admins
- Transparent listing of harvests with quantity and price
- Bulk booking workflow with status tracking
- Admin oversight to prevent fraud and ensure quality
- Audit log for full transaction transparency
